About Project Olympus

How the Model Works

Project Olympus scores all 48 WC 2026 teams on four structural dimensions, applies six squad-level modifiers, then runs 50,000 Monte Carlo simulations using a Dixon-Coles Poisson match engine. All four pillars feed into individual match simulations.

1
Data collection: 1,248 players (26 per team) from FBref, Transfermarkt, official sources. All rosters verified against FIFA June 2, 2026 squads. 38 stat columns per player.
2
4-pillar scoring: Each team scored across four weighted dimensions, normalised across all 48 teams. Club-based league quality multiplier applied to all player stats (Big 5 = 1.00, MLS = 0.78, Saudi PL = 0.68, Uzbek PL = 0.52).
3
6 modifier variables: Age curve, squad depth, 6-month form, fragility, penalty record, travel burden. Combined max effect +/-17 pts, individually capped.
4
Match engine: Dixon-Coles corrected Poisson. Attack driven by P2, boosted by P3 (tactics) and P4 (cohesion). Defence driven by P1, boosted by P4 (solidity) and P3 (organisation). All four pillars influence every result.
5
Bracket extraction: 50,000 independent tournament runs. Each slot records its most frequent winner to produce the most probable single-path bracket.

The Four Pillars

P1 — Macro Strength 28%
FIFA ranking, ELO rating, squad market value, historical WC performance, confederation multiplier. Primary driver of defensive strength in the match engine.
Sources: FIFA.com, ClubElo.com, Transfermarkt
P2 — Player Quality 34% (highest)
Individual player stats with club-based league quality multiplier. xG, xA, key passes, dribbles, duel success for outfield; save% and clean sheets for GK. Primary driver of attacking strength in the match engine.
Sources: FBref 2025-26, Transfermarkt, official league databases
P3 — Coaching 13%
International win rate, tenure, trophies, WC experience. Boosts attacking efficiency (tactical organisation) and contributes to defensive organisation in the match engine.
Sources: Soccerway, official federation sites
P4 — Auxiliary 25%
Squad cohesion, defensive efficiency, tactical flexibility, international experience. Primary boost to defensive solidity and secondary boost to attack through cohesion-driven chance creation.

WC 2026 Qualification & Advancement Rules

48 teams in 12 groups of 4. Top 2 per group advance automatically (24 teams). The 8 best third-placed teams across all 12 groups also advance, giving 32 teams in the Round of 32.

StageHow it works
Top 2 per groupAutomatic. Ranked by: (1) Points, (2) Goal difference, (3) Goals scored, (4) Head-to-head, (5) Drawing of lots.
Best 8 thirdsAll 12 third-placed teams ranked together by same criteria. Best 8 advance. Model score used as tiebreaker in simulations.
Round of 32Group winners face runners-up or best thirds. Bracket pairings follow the FIFA 2026 predetermined structure.
KnockoutsSingle elimination. Level after 90 min: 30 min extra time, then penalties. Model simulates shootouts using historical WC records per team.

The Six Modifier Variables

All normalised 0-100 across 48 teams. Score 50 = neutral. Hard cap per variable.

Age-Performance Curve max +/-4.0 pts
Squad age weighted by availability. Peak 26-29. Physical stats decay faster above 32, 34, 36. Veterans contribute at a discounted rate.
Squad Depth max +/-3.0 pts
How evenly quality is distributed across all 26 players. Star-dependent squads are vulnerable to injury and fatigue over 7 games.
6-Month Form max +/-4.0 pts
Opponent-weighted international results January–June 2026. Spain (92), Germany (88), Portugal (85) entered in excellent form.
Squad Fragility max +/-2.5 pts
% of total squad xG+xA concentrated in the top 3 players. Spain most balanced. Norway and Egypt highly star-dependent.
Penalty Record max +/-2.0 pts
Historical WC shootout record. Weights the penalty shootout simulation in knockout rounds. Germany all-time best.
Travel Burden max +/-1.5 pts
Group stage km between venues. England 0 km (all Vancouver). Croatia/Ghana/Panama 6,713 km across Group L. Smallest modifier.

Glossary

Final ScoreComposite model score after all 4 pillars and 6 modifiers. Relative strength index, not a probability.
Base Score4-pillar score before modifiers. Compare with Final Score to see modifier effect.
ModifierNet point change from all 6 variables. Capped at +6/-8.
WIN %Share of 50,000 simulations where this team won the World Cup.
ADV %Probability of advancing from the group stage (1st, 2nd, or one of 8 best thirds).
DHIDark Horse Index. Gap between simulated performance and model score prediction. High DHI = upset potential.
xGExpected Goals. Probability a shot results in a goal. More stable predictor than actual goals scored.
xAExpected Assists. xG value of shots directly created by a pass or cross.
Dixon-ColesCorrection to the Poisson model improving accuracy for low-scoring results (0-0, 1-0).
Monte CarloRunning thousands of random trials to estimate probabilities. Each of 50,000 runs is a fully independent tournament.
League QualityClub-based multiplier on player stats. Big 5 = 1.00, Brasileirao = 0.82, MLS = 0.78, Saudi PL = 0.68, Uzbek PL = 0.52.
ELOChess-derived football rating. More responsive to recent results than FIFA rankings. Used as P1 input.
Most Likely BracketSingle most frequent path across 50,000 simulations. Low winner % reflects genuine uncertainty, not model imprecision.

Model Limitations

What this model cannot capture

  • In-tournament momentum and psychology
  • Managerial in-game tactical adaptation
  • Injuries during the tournament
  • Referee decisions, VAR, or match-day conditions
  • Historically elevated WC performance vs club form
  • Team chemistry that builds during the tournament
  • Estimated stats for low-data leagues (Iraqi PL, Jordanian PL, Uzbek PL)
